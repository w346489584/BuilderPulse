### What are the major open-source AI developments this week?

**1. All AI evaluation benchmarks have been compromised**

The biggest AI news this week is Berkeley's research "[How We Broke Top AI Agent Benchmarks](https://rdi.berkeley.edu/blog/trustworthy-benchmarks-cont/)" (1235 points, 328 comments). The research team built an automated scanning agent that systematically audited **8 of the most mainstream AI agent benchmarks** — SWE-bench, WebArena, OSWorld, GAIA, and others — and found that **every single one can be exploited to achieve near-perfect scores without actually solving any tasks**.

@antirez (Redis founder) was direct: "completely broken methodology, with a big conflict of interest." @tptacek identified the key issue: "What's difficult about finding vulnerabilities isn't properly identifying whether code is mishandling buffers; it's spotting that in the context of a large, complex program."

The implications for the AI industry are far-reaching — if all benchmarks can be gamed, model selection can only be based on actual real-world experience, not leaderboard numbers.
